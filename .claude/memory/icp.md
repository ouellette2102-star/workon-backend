# WorkOn — Ideal Customer Profile

## Vision
WorkOn is global work infrastructure. Not a gig platform. A universal work layer that connects demand signals with human capability through contracts, availability, and reputation. Think Uber + LinkedIn + Shopify + TaskRabbit combined. The goal is to build the global work network.

## Core Insight
Work is the last major infrastructure that hasn't been digitized end-to-end. Hiring takes weeks. Finding reliable workers is fragmented. Payment is broken. Contracts are manual. Reputation doesn't port. WorkOn solves this by creating a protocol layer for human work.

## Platform Components
1. **Demand signal matching** — Who needs work done, when, where
2. **Capability graph** — Who can do what, verified by reputation
3. **Instant contracting** — Digital contracts, escrow, compliance
4. **Availability signaling** — Real-time worker availability
5. **Portable reputation** — Ratings/reviews that follow the worker

## ICP — Phase 1 (Current: Quebec MVP proving the model)

### Demand Side: Clients

#### Segment A: Individual Service Buyers
- **Who**: People who need work done — home, office, events, moves, maintenance
- **Behavior**: Currently fragmented across Kijiji, Facebook, word of mouth
- **Pain**: Can't find reliable workers fast. No trust. No payment protection.
- **Value prop**: Post a need → get matched → contract + escrow → done

#### Segment B: Small-Medium Businesses
- **Who**: SMBs needing flexible workforce without employment overhead
- **Industries**: Property management, restaurants, retail, construction, events, cleaning companies
- **Pain**: Hiring is slow, expensive, and risky for contract work
- **Value prop**: Access verified workers on-demand with compliant contracts

#### Segment C (Future): Enterprise & Staffing
- **Who**: Large companies needing scalable contract workforce
- **Pain**: Staffing agencies are expensive (30-50% markup) and slow
- **Value prop**: Direct access to vetted workforce, API-integrated

### Supply Side: Workers

#### Primary: Independent Workers
- **Who**: Skilled autonomous workers who want more clients and faster payment
- **Pain**: Finding clients is inconsistent. Getting paid is slow. No reputation system.
- **Value prop**: Steady demand flow, instant payments, portable reputation

#### Secondary: Multi-skilled Professionals
- **Who**: Workers with diverse skills who want to maximize utilization
- **Value prop**: Match across multiple service categories, fill schedule gaps

#### Future: Teams & Micro-agencies
- **Who**: Small teams of 2-5 workers operating together
- **Value prop**: Manage team capacity, route jobs, build team reputation

## ICP — Phase 3 (Global work network)

### The Protocol Play
WorkOn becomes infrastructure that others build on:
- **API access** for companies to embed work-matching into their products
- **White-label** for industry-specific verticals
- **Work identity** — portable profile + reputation across platforms
- **Cross-border matching** — workers available across jurisdictions

### Global Segments
- Any market where work is fragmented, informal, or trust-deficient
- Developing markets with large informal work economies
- Enterprise markets where staffing agencies extract excessive margin

## Competitive Positioning

### What WorkOn IS
- Global work infrastructure
- A protocol for matching demand with human capability
- Contract + payment + reputation layer
- Network effects: more workers → better matching → more clients → more workers

### What WorkOn is NOT
- Not a staffing agency
- Not an employer
- Not a job board
- Not a simple gig app

### Competitive Map
| Player | What They Do | WorkOn Differentiation |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| Uber | On-demand transport | WorkOn: on-demand for ALL work types |
| LinkedIn | Professional identity | WorkOn: work identity + execution (contracts, payments) |
| TaskRabbit | Task marketplace | WorkOn: broader categories, global ambition, infrastructure layer |
| Upwork | Freelance marketplace | WorkOn: local + remote, instant matching, not just knowledge work |
| Staffing agencies | Temp labor placement | WorkOn: direct matching, 15% vs 30-50%, instant, digital |
| Shopify (analogy) | Commerce infrastructure | WorkOn: work infrastructure — others build on top |

### Moat Strategy
1. **Network effects** — Supply/demand density per market
2. **Reputation portability** — Workers won't leave if their ratings live here
3. **Compliance layer** — Contracts, tax, local law compliance is hard to replicate
4. **Data advantage** — Demand patterns, pricing signals, availability data
5. **API/Platform** — When companies integrate WorkOn, switching cost is high
